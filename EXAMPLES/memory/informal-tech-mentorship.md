# Informal Tech Mentorship

Source: https://basis.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/BET/pages/35815632

## Introduction

An informal tech mentorship relationship is meant to provide an additional resource for the mentee outside of their traditional manager/reporting structure. The mentee should feel comfortable talking to their mentor about tech and non-tech topics, Basis and non-Basis, or really any topic that's on their mind. There is no set timeline, the meetings can continue as long as both parties feel they are valuable. By building such a relationship, the hope is that the mentee builds a solid foundation in the Basis community, from which they can build other relationships.

Compared to the , a tech mentorship generally has less structure and is more ad-hoc. This doesn't diminish the importance of the relationship, but the two parties should discuss and agree upon a cadence that best suits the needs of the mentee's career growth. It also depends on the mentee's interests and career goals.

## Guidelines

A few of these match the more formal Mentorship parent document:

  * This mentorship does not need to be primarily technical; the new hire has a manager and a team lead who can develop a technical onboarding plan. The mentor should definitely be available as a technical resource and should check in on how the technical side of things is going, but the primary goal should be to help with questions about team and company culture, company organization and history, context within the organization, and other such issues.
  * If there is mutual interest around a side project/book club/podcast/screencast, etc., definitely take advantage of the opportunity.  Both parties can benefit from this, the Mentor has an opportunity to grow from this as well as the Mentee.
  * Over time as both participants grow, the types of topics are expected to evolve

## Example Topics of Discussion

  * Interesting tech talks unrelated to Cento's chosen technologies (e.g. NoSQL databases, alternative programming languages)
  * Hobbies, both technical and non-technical (e.g. board games, sports, arts & crafts, extracurriculars)
  * Checking in on how the mentee is feeling about their work, team, stress, etc.
  * Future learning and/or other non-work-related goals (e.g. life goals, parenthood)
  * Mentor's past experience at other companies, industries, tech stacks, company sizes, and how Centro compares

## Summary

This mentorship process is a flexible, ever-changing thing that should be adapted over time. How that will look will depend on the interests of both participants, but also be beneficial to both.
